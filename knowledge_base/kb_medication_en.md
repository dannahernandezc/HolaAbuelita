# Medication adherence in older adults — reference guide

## Purpose
This guide helps classify the importance of responses related to taking medication. It does not replace medical or pharmacist guidance.

## Why it matters
Skipping doses of medication for chronic conditions (blood pressure, diabetes, heart conditions, among others) can have cumulative effects that aren't visible right away but increase risk over time. Adherence is one of the best early indicators of an older adult's overall well-being.

## Normal level
- "Yes, already took them"
- "Yes, as usual"
- "I don't take any medication" (if this is consistent with the person's usual profile)

## Moderate attention level
- "No, I forgot" (mentioned once)
- "No, I ran out, need to buy more"
- "Yes, but later than usual"
- Recent medication changes mentioned without further detail

## Urgent level
- "No" for two or more consecutive days
- Mention of strong side effects (intense dizziness, fainting, bleeding)
- Confusion about which medication or dose is correct
- "I ran out and don't know when I can buy more" combined with a medication for a major chronic condition

## Note for the agent
A single isolated missed dose is generally not urgent, but two consecutive misses are: the pattern matters more than the individual event. If the person explicitly mentions a medication related to the heart, blood pressure, or diabetes, raise the priority level compared to other occasional-use medications.
